## 4. Success Story Framework

Template for a future `/case-studies/:slug` or `/stories/:slug` page. Use this structure when collecting and publishing real user success stories.

---

### Page Structure

#### Section 1: The Hook (1 sentence)
One-line version of the outcome. Should include: company interviewed at (or company type), role, and result.

> Example: "How [Name] landed a senior PM role at [Company] with 72 hours of prep."

---

#### Section 2: The Situation (2–3 sentences)
Who is the person? What was their background? What made this interview challenging for them specifically?

> Example: "After 6 years in operations, [Name] was making a career pivot into product management. She had one shot at a final-round interview at [Company] — a company she'd applied to twice before — with three days to prepare."

---

#### Section 3: The Prep (what they did)
What did they do with PrepFile? Which sections of the brief did they use? What did they learn that they wouldn't have found otherwise?

Structure:
- Brief generation: company, role, inputs
- What the brief surfaced that was new or unexpected
- How they used it to adjust their prep strategy

---

#### Section 4: The Interview
What happened in the room (or on the call)? Did anything from the brief come up? Did they use company-specific context or questions from the brief?

---

#### Section 5: The Result
Did they get the offer? What happened next? Optional: what would they have done differently without PrepFile?

---

#### Section 6: The Quote
Pull a 2–3 sentence quote that captures the core value in their own voice. This becomes the testimonial used in landing page rotation.

---

### User Interview Questions (for collecting success stories)

Ask these during a 20-minute call or async via email:

1. What role were you interviewing for, and at what type of company?
2. What made this interview particularly challenging for you going in?
3. How did you come across PrepFile, and how much prep time did you have?
4. What did you input, and what stood out to you in the brief?
5. Did anything from the brief come up in the actual interview?
6. What was the outcome?
7. How would you describe the experience to a friend who was prepping for a big interview?
8. Is there anything you'd want other candidates in your situation to know?

---

### Consent and Attribution

Before publishing any case study:
- Confirm name, role, and company type the user is comfortable sharing
- Get written consent for direct quotes
- Offer option to use first name + last initial and company type instead of full name + company
- Send draft for approval before publishing
